Canada's National Cross-Disability Organization

Equality, Rights, and Inclusion for All Disabled Canadians.

The Council of Canadians with Disabilities advocates for a Canada where people with disabilities participate fully and equally in all aspects of life. We work with disabled people, by disabled people.

What We Do

Three pillars of disability-led change.

CCD's work is organized around three interconnected areas — each driven by the lived experience of disabled Canadians and grounded in human rights principles.

Human Rights & Justice

CCD uses strategic litigation, legal interventions, and rights-based policy analysis to advance the full recognition of disabled Canadians under domestic and international law. From Charter challenges to UN treaty monitoring, we fight for enforceable, meaningful rights.

Explore Human Rights & Justice

Community-Led Research

Our research programs centre disabled people as experts and knowledge creators. Through community-led studies, data collection, and evidence briefs, CCD generates the knowledge needed to drive systemic change in Canadian disability policy.

Explore Community-Led Research

Advocacy & Convenings

CCD convenes the disability community, engages with Parliament and federal regulators, and builds coalitions to advance accessibility, inclusion, and equity. We ensure the voices of disabled Canadians shape the policies that affect their lives.
